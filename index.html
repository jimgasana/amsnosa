Frequently Asked Questions
What is nuclear energy and how does it work?
Nuclear energy is produced by splitting uranium atoms in a controlled process called fission. The heat released is used to produce steam, which drives turbines to generate electricity. No burning of fuel occurs, so there are no air pollution emissions during operation. A small modular reactor the size of a few buildings can power over one million homes continuously.
Is nuclear power safe for Rwanda?
Modern small modular reactors are designed with passive safety systems that do not require power or human action to cool down in an emergency. Rwanda is selecting technologies with the strongest safety records available. The program will follow all International Atomic Energy Agency safety standards and will be independently regulated by the Rwanda Atomic Energy Board.
What happens to the nuclear waste?
The volume of waste produced by nuclear energy is very small compared to other fuels. All spent fuel is solid and can be safely stored in sealed containers. Rwanda's program will include a national waste management plan with long-term solutions that are common practice in countries that have operated nuclear power for decades.
How will this affect electricity prices for households?
Nuclear energy has very high upfront costs but very low and stable operational costs. Over the long term, nuclear power provides predictable electricity prices that are not affected by fuel price fluctuations. The program is designed to reduce Rwanda's dependence on imported fossil fuels and stabilize energy costs for industry and homes.
How can communities participate?
Public engagement is a formal requirement of the program. Community consultation sessions, public comment periods on environmental studies, and a dedicated information center will be established near any proposed site. All project data, contracts, and financial information will be published on this platform for public review.
